I am a Ph.D. student in Geographic Information Science (GIS) at University of Wisconsin - Madison, advised by Prof. Qunying Huang. I am also in the master program of Electrical and Computer Engineering (machine learning track) at UW-Madison.

I've been thinking about leveraging the power of statistical learning, machine learning, optimization, and computer vision to advance remote sensing image understanding for social good applications.

Previously, I got the M.Sc. from Institute of Remote Sensing and Digital Earth, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (UCAS) (2017), and the B.Eng. from School of Remote Sensing and Information Enginnering, Wuhan University (2014).

Research

My research interests span machine learning, computer vision, and remote sensing. I am recently focusing on self-supervised learning of spatial-spectral features in satellite and aerial imagery for disaster response.
